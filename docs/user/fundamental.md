# Fundamental Analysis — User Guide

## What does it do?
Answers the question: "Is this company actually a good business and is it priced fairly?" Uses the same metrics professional analysts use — valuation ratios, growth rates, and financial health scores.

## Tabs

### Valuation
How expensive is the stock relative to its fundamentals?

| Metric | What it means | Rule of thumb |
|---|---|---|
| **PE Ratio** | Price per $1 of earnings | < 15 = cheap, > 30 = expensive (for large caps) |
| **PB Ratio** | Price per $1 of book value | < 1 = potentially undervalued |
| **PS Ratio** | Price per $1 of revenue | Useful for unprofitable growth companies |
| **EV/EBITDA** | Enterprise value vs operating profit | < 10 = reasonable for most sectors |
| **Graham Number** | Benjamin Graham's intrinsic value formula | Stock trading below = margin of safety |
| **DCF Estimate** | Discounted cash flow intrinsic value | Directional only — very sensitive to assumptions |

### Growth Score
Rates the company's growth momentum from 0–100:
- Revenue growth (year-over-year and 3-year average)
- Earnings growth
- Margin expansion or contraction
- Free cash flow yield

**70+** = strong growth profile. **Below 40** = slowing or declining.

### Quality Score (Financial Health)
Rates how financially sound the company is from 0–100:

| Metric | What it tells you |
|---|---|
| **ROE** | How efficiently the company uses shareholder money |
| **ROA** | How efficiently it uses all its assets |
| **D/E Ratio** | How much debt vs equity — lower is safer |
| **Current Ratio** | Can it pay its short-term bills? > 1.5 = healthy |
| **Altman Z-Score** | Bankruptcy risk model — > 3.0 = safe, < 1.8 = distressed |
| **Piotroski F-Score** | 9-point checklist of financial improvement signals — 8–9 = strong |

**70+** quality score = financially healthy company.

### Screener
Filter 50 large-cap stocks by PE, ROE, profitability, and get them ranked by combined growth + quality score.

## How to use it
Think of it as a three-layer filter:
1. **Is the stock cheap?** → Valuation tab (look for PE below sector average)
2. **Is the business growing?** → Growth score > 60
3. **Is the balance sheet safe?** → Quality score > 60, D/E < 1.0

A stock that scores well on all three is a potential value + quality compounder.

## Important caveat
Fundamental analysis tells you about the business — not the timing. A great company can still be a bad trade if bought at the wrong price or into a deteriorating macro environment. Use it alongside Technical Analysis and Options flow for better timing.
