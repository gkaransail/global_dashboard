# Insider Tracker — User Guide

## What does it do?
Tracks what company executives, directors, and major shareholders are doing with their own stock — from SEC Form 4 filings (which are legally required disclosures). Insider buying is one of the most powerful signals in markets because insiders know more about the business than anyone else.

## Tabs

### Transaction Feed
Shows every recent insider transaction for your selected ticker: who bought/sold, how many shares, at what price, and what they're worth today.

**Fields explained:**
- **Insider / Title** — CEO, CFO, Director, 10% Owner, etc.
- **Transaction Type** — Purchase = bullish signal. Sale = weaker (insiders sell for many reasons)
- **Price / Value** — Price paid per share × shares = total value of the transaction
- **P&L %** — How much the insider is up/down since their trade

### Cluster Buys
The most powerful feature. Scans hundreds of stocks and flags ones where **multiple different insiders are buying at the same time** within a rolling window. Cluster buying = insiders collectively believe something positive is coming.

**Parameters you can adjust:**
- **Min insiders** — Minimum distinct buyers to flag (2+ is standard, 3+ is a strong signal)
- **Look-back window** — How far back to search
- **Cluster window** — How tight the buying cluster must be (smaller window = more coordinated)

## How to interpret signals

| Signal | Strength | Why |
|---|---|---|
| CEO buying + large dollar amount | Very strong | CEOs rarely risk personal capital unless highly confident |
| Multiple insiders buying together | Very strong | Cluster buying suggests shared knowledge of upside |
| Director buying on open market | Strong | Directors have fiduciary duty — they take it seriously |
| Insider selling | Weak | Insiders sell for taxes, diversification, lifestyle — not necessarily bearish |
| Automatic plan sales (10b5-1) | Neutral | Pre-planned, not discretionary |

## Key rules of thumb
- **Focus on purchases, not sales.** Insider selling has many explanations; buying has one — they expect the price to go up.
- **Dollar size matters.** A CEO buying $50,000 of stock when they make $5M/year is routine. A CEO buying $2M is conviction.
- **Cluster > individual.** One insider could be wrong. Three insiders buying in the same month = high confidence signal.
- **SEC filings are not real-time.** Companies have 2 business days to file Form 4, so data is slightly delayed.
