# 13F Holdings (Institutional) — User Guide

## What does it do?
Shows what the "smart money" — hedge funds, mutual funds, pension funds, and other large institutions — owns and how their positions are changing. Institutions manage billions and do deep research before buying. Tracking their moves is a form of crowd-sourced due diligence.

## Tabs

### Top Holders
Lists the largest institutional shareholders for your selected stock:
- **Institution name** — Vanguard, BlackRock, Fidelity, Tiger Global, etc.
- **Shares held** — Absolute position size
- **% of Outstanding** — What fraction of total shares they own
- **Change** — Did they add or reduce their position last quarter?
- **Flow label** — Accumulating / Distributing / Unchanged

### Fund Flow
A summary of the collective institutional sentiment:
- **Net flow** — Are more institutions adding vs reducing?
- **Total institutional ownership %** — High ownership (>70%) means institutions dominate the stock
- **Buyers vs Sellers** — How many institutions are adding vs reducing last quarter

### Screener
Finds stocks with a specific institutional ownership profile:
- **Min institutional ownership** — e.g., only show stocks where institutions own >50%
- **Flow direction** — Only show stocks where institutions are net accumulating

## How to interpret

| Signal | What it means |
|---|---|
| High ownership + accumulating | Smart money is increasing bets — bullish |
| High ownership + distributing | Smart money is exiting — caution |
| Very high ownership (>80%) | Few shares left for retail to drive up; also means institutions control the price |
| Low ownership + new positions | Early institutional discovery — potentially high upside |
| Concentrated ownership (1-2 funds own >20%) | Single fund's decision can cause big moves |

## Important limitations
- **13F filings are quarterly and delayed.** You see what institutions owned at the end of a quarter, disclosed 45 days later. By the time you see it, they may have already changed positions.
- **13F shows long positions only.** Shorts, options, and foreign holdings are not reported.
- **Not all institutions are equally informed.** Index funds like Vanguard hold everything — their positions mean less than a concentrated active manager.
- **Best for confirming conviction**, not generating new ideas.
