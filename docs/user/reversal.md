# Reversal Scanner — User Guide

## What does it do?
The Reversal Scanner looks at a stock from multiple angles — price momentum, volume patterns, market sentiment, and macro conditions — and gives you a directional read: is this stock more likely to go up or down from here?

## Tabs

### Single Stock
Enter any ticker and get an instant directional signal.

**Reading the result:**
- **Direction** — Bullish (up), Bearish (down), or Neutral
- **Confidence** — How strongly the signals agree (higher = more conviction)
- **Strength** — Weak / Moderate / Strong / Very Strong
- **Signal breakdown** — Each individual signal that contributed, with an explanation

**Tip:** Enable "Explain" to see why each signal fired.

### Sector Scan
Ranks all 11 market sectors by their reversal signal. Use this to find which sectors are showing the strongest momentum shifts — rotate into leading sectors, avoid lagging ones.

### Watchlist
Runs the reversal analysis on all tickers in your watchlist simultaneously, sorted by confidence. Useful for your morning scan.

### Macro View
Shows the 5-day and 20-day returns for key macro indicators:
- **DXY** — US Dollar strength
- **VIX** — Market fear (high = fearful, consider contrarian buys)
- **Gold** — Safe haven demand
- **WTI Oil** — Energy market signal
- **10Y Yield** — Interest rate direction
- **Copper** — Global economic health

## How to use it
1. Use **Single Stock** for conviction — look for confidence > 0.65 with ≥5 aligned signals
2. Check **Sector Scan** first — fish in the right pond
3. Run **Watchlist** every morning to rank your opportunities for the day
4. Cross-reference with **Options Analysis** for confirmation (options flow + reversal = stronger signal)

## What it does NOT do
- It does not predict the exact price or timing
- It does not account for binary events (earnings, FDA decisions)
- Signals are based on historical patterns — past patterns do not guarantee future moves
