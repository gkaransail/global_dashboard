# Fear & Greed Index — User Guide

## What does it do?
Measures the current emotional temperature of the stock market on a scale from 0 (Extreme Fear) to 100 (Extreme Greed). Helps you understand whether the market is panicking (potential buying opportunity) or euphoric (time for caution).

## The Composite Score

| Range | Label | What it typically means |
|---|---|---|
| 0–24 | Extreme Fear | Market is panicking — historically a buying opportunity |
| 25–44 | Fear | Caution and defensive positioning dominate |
| 45–55 | Neutral | Balanced sentiment |
| 56–74 | Greed | Risk appetite is elevated |
| 75–100 | Extreme Greed | Euphoria — historically a warning sign |

## The 6 Sub-Indicators

### VIX (Volatility Fear Gauge)
The CBOE Volatility Index measures how much the options market expects the S&P 500 to move. VIX above 30 = fear. VIX below 15 = complacency.

### Market Momentum
Compares the S&P 500 to its 125-day moving average. When the market is well above its long-term average, greed is driving it. Below = fear selling.

### Put/Call Ratio
Tracks how many puts (bets on a decline) vs calls (bets on a rise) are being bought. High put/call = fear. Low = greed.

### Safe Haven Demand
When investors flee stocks for bonds and gold, it signals fear. When they prefer stocks over safe havens, it signals greed.

### Junk Bond Spread
The extra yield investors demand to hold risky corporate bonds vs government bonds. Wide spreads = fear (investors demand more compensation for risk).

### Market Breadth
What percentage of S&P 500 stocks are above their 50-day moving average. Low breadth = the rally is narrow and fragile (fear). High breadth = broad participation (greed).

## How to use it

**Contrarian strategy:** Extreme Fear often precedes market bottoms — when everyone has sold, there are few sellers left. Extreme Greed often precedes corrections.

**Not a timing tool:** The market can stay fearful or greedy for extended periods. Use this as one input alongside technical and fundamental analysis.

**Best used for:** Position sizing (reduce exposure in Extreme Greed, increase in Extreme Fear) and checking if your individual stock picks are swimming against or with the tide.
